About Westcliff Hard

Established in 1929, Westcliff Hardcourt Tennis Club is the foremost club in the area.

'Tennis for everyone’ is our motto. We have playing standards to suit every type of player, from beginner to Essex League standard. Most of the best players in the area play at our club, and we also have programmes that cater for adult beginners as well as programmes for children as young as fives years old. We cover the whole spectrum.

Westliff Hard is perhaps most famous for being the club where the Lloyd brothers spent their formative years as players. David, John and Tony Lloyd all played at Wimbledon in the 1970s and are indeed still members of Westcliff Hard today.

Achievements

Westcliff Hard has achieved a proud record of success:

  • It is the leading club in the Southend area;
  • It is one of the top 2-3 clubs in Essex;
  • It competes for National championships.

National Competitions

Westcliff Hard men won the National Club Championships in 1981 beating the prestigious Queens Club in the final. The club currently competes in the Aegon National Singles League.

County Competitions

Westcliff Hard men won the Home Counties cup in 1972, only the second team from Essex to have done so.

In the senior Essex county competitions:

  • The Men have won the Senior League Div.I 13 times & the Senior Cup 7 times.
  • The Ladies have won the Senior League Div.I 6 times & the Senior Cup for the last 2 years).
  • Other mens, ladies, veterans and junior teams, including 2nd and 3rd teams, have won many other county cup & league events.

District Tournaments

In the Southend & District competitions the club has won countless titles.

In Leigh & Westcliff competitions the club fields a variety of teams in mens, ladies, mixed and veterans leagues, at different standards, and has achieved consistent success with a view to being competitive rather than dominant. We try and give everyone a game.

Individual Sucess

At the individual level the club has had outstanding success:

  • John Lloyd – the last British player before Andy Murray to reach a grand slam singles final.
  • David Lloyd – semi-finalist Wimbledon Mens doubles & final of the Davis Cup.
  • Tony Lloyd – Wimbledon singles player.
  • Ian Currie – represented England.
